Introduction

These are instructions for how to create a breakout room within a Zoom Meeting. These instructions are intended for users who have never used Zoom before, and will cover everything from the installation of the Zoom client to creating a meeting to opening breakout rooms. These instructions will define and explain Zoom terminology, and walk the reader through the process of creating a breakout room in a linear, step by step fashion.

Zoom is a video calling software often used to facilitate online classes. Breakout rooms are a feature in Zoom that allow the host of the meeting to split the meeting participants into their own smaller, private “rooms”, making it a useful feature for things like group work.

These instructions are for professors and instructors who intend to use Zoom for their virtual classroom, and want to create breakout rooms to facilitate group work. The reader should be able to teach others how to maneuver Zoom breakout rooms after following our set of instructions.

In order to use these instructions, it is required to have a computer/digital technology that is capable of running the Zoom application. See more details in the “List of materials and equipment needed” section.

These set of instructions are intended to take the stress off of students and professors during an already challenging time in the middle of a global pandemic. The official documentation for the Zoom application is often broad and confusing for an average user. It is difficult enough for some to learn, and with the addition of a new “virtual classroom” this can hinder a student’s academic performance.

List of materials and equipment needed

To run the Zoom application, you will need a computer with a supported operating system and Internet connection. You’ll also need a webcam and microphone to participate in video calls.

Zoom supports most operating systems, including:

  • Windows (7, 8, 8.1, 10)
  • macOS (10.9 or greater)
  • Linux (6.4 or higher)

A full list of supported operating systems can be found here.

Directions

Section 1: Installing the Zoom client

If you have already installed the zoom client, click here.

  1. Go to zoom.us/download and download Zoom Client for Meetings.

  1. Once the Zoom Installer is downloaded, click it to launch the installer.

You’ll find the installer in whatever folder your downloads go to. Let the installer run.

  1. Click “sign in”. If you already have a Zoom account, you can log in with your credentials. Otherwise, click “Sign Up Free” in the bottom right to make an account. Follow the instructions on the Zoom website to make an account, and then log in with those credentials.
  1. Before you can start a meeting, you need to enable breakout rooms in your account settings. Once you’re logged in, click the gear in the upper right to open your settings.

5. In the settings window, click “View More Settings” at the bottom. This will open the Zoom Web Client in your browser.

6. Scroll down to “In Meeting (Advanced)”, and switch “Breakout Room” to on.

 

If this switch is in the “off” position, you won’t be able to create Breakout Rooms.

Section 2: Starting a meeting

7. Click “New Meeting. A meeting window will open.

8. Click “Join With Computer Audio” to be able to hear others in the meeting. This will also allow the people in your meeting to hear you speak.

Section 3: Opening breakout rooms

9. This is what your meeting window will look like. To open breakout rooms, click the “Breakout Rooms” button on the bottom bar, towards the right.

10. This window should show up. Here, you can decide how you’d like to split the breakout rooms.

  • If you want participants to automatically be assigned to rooms at random, select “assign automatically.
  • If you want to choose which participants go into which rooms, select “assign manually”.
  • If you want to make rooms without assigning participants, and let participants choose which room they go into, select “let participants choose rooms”.

Assign Automatically

If you select “assign automatically”, this window will open with participants automatically assigned to rooms.

From here, you can adjust the participants by clicking the blue number at the right and deselecting the box next to the name to remove the participant from a room, or selecting the box next to the name to add the participant to a room.

►If you're happy with how the participants are assigned, click here to advance.

Assign Manually

If you select “assign manually”, you can choose which participants go into which rooms by clicking the “assign’ button and selecting the participants you want in that room.

►If you're happy with how the participants are assigned, click here to advance.

Let Participants Choose Rooms

If you select “allow participants to choose room”, participants will be able to click the Breakout Rooms button at the bottom of their screen and decide which room they want to join.

This option is useful if you want to let students choose their own groups.

►If you're happy with how the participants are assigned, click here to advance.

Other stuff about making rooms

You can rename or delete rooms by hovering over their bar.

If you want to start over the breakout room process, hit “recreate” to start from scratch.

14. Once you’re happy with which participants are in which rooms, click “Open All Rooms” to start the breakout rooms.

You can click “options” in the bottom left to adjust the Breakout Room options.

15. To end the breakout rooms, hit “Close All Rooms”.
